Sinnar

Sinnar is a town in the Nashik district of Maharashtra, India. As of the 2011 census, it had a population of approximately 65,299 residents. The town is renowned for the Gondeshwar Temple, an 11th-12th century temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, exemplifying the Hemadpanti style of architecture.
